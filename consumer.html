Consumer-Safe Language
No medical claims, no outcomes, no "weight loss." Language says "works with your body's natural signals" and "prescribed alongside your diet and exercise plan." The 3-step mechanism uses plain English, not biochemistry. No dosing information.
Warm Design Choices
Rounded corners everywhere (vs sharp edges on physician sheet). Olive/sage palette feels natural and wellness-oriented. Botanical SVG accent adds organic warmth. Gradient hero with soft gold/cream tones. The vial is smaller and secondary to the educational content.
Deliberately Omitted
No pricing. No SKU. No dosing protocols. No brand names (Mounjaro/Zepbound). No clinical trial numbers on front. No CAS number or molecular data. These are physician-only details. The consumer sees what they need: what it is, how it works, talk to your doctor.
Accessibility
Reading level targets 8th grade. No Latin/Greek terms without explanation. "Incretin mimetics" is introduced in context with a simple definition. Visual step-by-step mechanism replaces the dense receptor binding box from the physician version.

What Is Tirzepatide?
Tirzepatide is a prescription compound that works with two of your body's natural hormone pathways (called GIP and GLP-1). Your healthcare provider may prescribe it as part of a comprehensive treatment plan.
How Does It Work?
Your body naturally produces hormones called incretins that help regulate appetite and metabolism. Tirzepatide is designed to work with these same pathways, supporting your body's natural processes.
GIP Pathway
Engages your body's glucose-dependent insulinotropic polypeptide receptor
GLP-1 Pathway
Works with your glucagon-like peptide-1 receptor to support fullness signals
What Does the Research Say?
Published Clinical Research
Researchers have published results from large clinical trials evaluating tirzepatide in peer-reviewed medical journals. Your prescriber can discuss how these findings may relate to your individual situation.
Sources: N Engl J Med, 2022 • JCI Insight, 2020
Important Safety Information
Nausea Often improves over time
Digestive changes Discuss with your doctor
